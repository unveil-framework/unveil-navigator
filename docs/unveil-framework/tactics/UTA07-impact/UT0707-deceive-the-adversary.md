# UT0707 - Deceive the adversary

**Tactic:** [UTA07 - Impact](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may have the goal of providing false information to the source in order to deceive an adversary, aiming to disrupt their judgment about a specific issue and, therefore, their decision making process. The difference between the technique in Control “UT0619 - Test the source” and this technique is that, in UT0619, the handler's goal is to check if their control over the source is intact and if they can continue working with them, whereas the goal of UT0707 is to feed false information to an adversary via the source.

The handler is trying to report all the information they obtained during the encounter or interaction with a source, archiving it correctly. Although some of the information recovered from human interactions is assumed to remain tacit, online handling has the advantage of the information already being digitized to some extent. This is remarkable since while the recording of a conversation in the real world can be considered an intrusive and aggressive behavior, in online messaging apps it is assumed the “feature” of textual conversations are being logged by both sides. In this regard, it is paramount to pay attention and keep track of the way in which the information is collected and stored. In many cases, this tactic will (and should) coexist with preexisting approaches of conventional real-life human handling.
