# UT0907 - Alter memory

**Tactic:** [UTA09 - Exit](./README.md)

## Description

The handler employs techniques designed to alter or reshape the source’s memories of their interactions or the nature of their relationship. This could involve suggesting alternative narratives or contexts for events that occurred, effectively creating a less accurate but potentially less harmful memory of their involvement. This is particularly useful in sensitive operations where the source’s accurate recall of events could pose a risk.

The handler is trying to detect indicators of suspicious activity of the source, as well as to respond to said indicators in such a way that protects them and the overall operation. The main difference of this tactic when compared to the previous nine is that, while the rest are proactive, UTA10 is reactive, meaning that it includes possible ways in which the handler can react to unexpected behaviors of the source. This tactic includes what in other TTPs frameworks is dubbed mitigations or “blue” actions, as it comprises the defensive actions a handler can take when the source is or seems to be trying to influence, control or obtain anything from the handler.
