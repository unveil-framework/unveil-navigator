# UT0214.004 - Use cryptocurrencies: P2P physical exchanges

**Tactic:** [UTA02 - Technical Safeguarding](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may conduct peer-to-peer (P2P) cryptocurrency exchanges in a physical, face-toface setting. In this method, the handler meets with another individual in person to exchange cryptocurrency for cash or other assets, completely bypassing online platforms. This approach is highly advantageous for maintaining operational security and anonymity, as it eliminates digital traces that could be monitored or recorded by adversaries or surveillance systems. Physical P2P exchanges are often conducted in discrete locations to ensure privacy and reduce the risk of interception or observation. The handler must carefully select trustworthy counterparts to avoid scams or potential security threats during the transaction. Additionally, this method allows for the exchange of cryptocurrencies without the need for intermediaries, KYC processes, or any online interaction, significantly enhancing the operation’s security by reducing exposure to digital surveillance. By utilizing physical P2P exchanges, the handler can acquire, dispose of or trade cryptocurrencies with minimal risk of traceability, ensuring that the financial aspect of the operation remains covert and secure.
