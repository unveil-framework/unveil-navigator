# UT0602.001 - Use incentives: Promise reinforcements

**Tactic:** [UTA06 - Control](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may promise rewards to the source in order to increase the probability of them carrying out the behavior the handler wants. In this sense, a reinforcement is anything that increases the probability of a behavior in the source and, thus, will depend on their personality and motivational profile. Reinforcements can be both positive (giving something that the source likes, like a praise or money) or negative (taking away something the source does not like, such as a feeling of anxiety or something that causes physical pain118). By offering incentives, such as praise, recognition, privileges or material rewards, the handler aims to motivate the source to engage in behaviors that demonstrate trust and cooperation. It is essential for the handler to follow through on promised reinforcements to maintain credibility and reinforce trust in the relationship. For example, a simple way of giving a positive reinforcement is by using active listening techniques119. The handler may show they are actively listening to make the source feel more understood, making them develop a higher trust on the handler. This technique can be easily generated by avoiding interrupting, actively acting as if the handler is listening (nodding in video communication, sending little messages such as “oh, I get it” while the source is writing via text), asking clarifying questions, etc.
