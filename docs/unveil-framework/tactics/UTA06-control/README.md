# UTA06 - Control

## Description

The handler is trying to establish and maintain control over the source and the collection
process. In this context, “to control” a source refers to the process of managing the relationship
between the handler and the source, as well as the interactions between them. Therefore, the
main goal of the handler during this tactic is to ensure that they are the one leading the source
and not the other way around, which includes designing, representing and adapting a specific
relationship with them. By no means the handler should lose control of the relationship and
allow the source to dictate its terms, even if the strategy chosen by the handler and their team
includes making the source feel in control, or even acting submissive to them.
This tactic must be taken into account during the whole of the relationship, not only when
approaching the source for the first time. Effective control also ensures that the handler can
steer the interaction to meet strategic objectives while minimizing risks. It must be noted that
the techniques in this tactic are designed to be executed combined with the ones in the UT05 Influence tactic.
