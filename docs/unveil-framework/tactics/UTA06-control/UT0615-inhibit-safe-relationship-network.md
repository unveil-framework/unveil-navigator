# UT0615 - Inhibit safe relationship network

**Tactic:** [UTA06 - Control](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may actively work to inhibit the source's ability to establish or maintain a safe relationship network outside of the handler's influence. This can be achieved by discouraging the source from confiding in or seeking support from individuals or groups that could potentially undermine the handler's control over the source. It may involve subtle manipulation tactics aimed at eroding trust in external relationships or creating dependency on the handler for emotional or logistical support. In contexts such as insider threat investigations or intelligence gathering within hostile environments, the handler might want to ensure that the source does not leak information or seek advice from external parties that could compromise the operation. By subtly undermining the source's trust in their existing network—such as colleagues, friends, or online communities —the handler can inhibit the source's ability to form or maintain supportive relationships outside of the handler's influence. This isolation makes the source more reliant on the handler for emotional and logistical support, thereby increasing the handler's control over the source and ensuring a steady flow of valuable information without external interference.
