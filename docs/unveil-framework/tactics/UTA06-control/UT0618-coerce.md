# UT0618 - Coerce

**Tactic:** [UTA06 - Control](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may resort to coercion tactics to maintain control over the relationship with the source. Coercion involves the use of threats or intimidation to compel the source to comply with the handler's directives. This technique aims to instill fear or apprehension in the source, thereby influencing their behavior and cooperation. It must be noted that, although effective in some situations, this technique has negative consequences in long-term relationships, and should therefore be carefully applied to continuous and formal contacts, as it could undermine the trust in the handler. Moreover, it should be used with care in elicitation operations, as the source can not know they are dealing, collaborating with or being coerced by an intelligence agency.
