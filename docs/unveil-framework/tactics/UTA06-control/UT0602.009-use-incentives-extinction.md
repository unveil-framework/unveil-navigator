# UT0602.009 - Use incentives: Extinction

**Tactic:** [UTA06 - Control](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may employ extinction as a behavioral technique by withholding previously provided reinforcements or rewards in response to undesired behaviors exhibited by the source124. By eliminating the positive outcomes associated with these behaviors, the handler aims to decrease their occurrence, ultimately shaping the source's behavior towards more desirable patterns. This technique relies on the principle that behaviors not reinforced will diminish over time, as long as it is ensured that the chosen response is not being reinforced in any way. However, it must be noted that withholding a reinforcement will cause an increase of the undesired behavior, as a last resort to regain the reinforcement.
