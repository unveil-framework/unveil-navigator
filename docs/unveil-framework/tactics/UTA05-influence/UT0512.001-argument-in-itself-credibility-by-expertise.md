# UT0512.001 - Argument in itself: Credibility by expertise

**Tactic:** [UTA05 - Influence](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may establish their credibility by demonstrating their expertise or knowledge in relevant subjects. This can be achieved by sharing relevant qualifications, experiences, or successes in areas related to the conversation topic. By showcasing expertise, the handler aims to increase the source's receptiveness to their message. Expertise can be real or fake, and it can be displayed in different ways: academic titles, verbal fluidity, technical language, display of data, a white coat, etc. It must be noted that, even if in scientific studies expertise and authority might be considered different variables, in a real-life context expertise, whether real or faked, might lead the source into thinking that person has some kind of authority or power. However, it must be noted that authority may not have the same effect on every source, so it is highly relevant to understand how each individual feels about it, as authority figures may trigger trauma, generate rejection, anger or other negative feelings for some people, while for others it can be attractive.
