# UT0508.002 - Motivational tactics: BAS

**Tactic:** [UTA05 - Influence](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may employ strategies to activate the Behavioral Activation System (BAS) in the source. BAS is linked to approach behaviors and sensitivity to rewards. The handler seeks to stimulate enthusiasm, excitement or anticipation in the source by emphasizing the potential benefits, rewards or positive outcomes. This can involve highlighting opportunities for personal gain, advancement or fulfillment that may result from cooperation. For example, the handler could engage the source in a conversation that subtly emphasizes the excitement and prestige associated with access to exclusive information or elite networks. By painting a vivid picture of the benefits—such as career advancement, financial gain or the thrill of being an insider—the handler can stimulate the source's enthusiasm and anticipation. The handler might also use flattery and personal attention to make the source feel uniquely valued and appreciated, further increasing their motivation to collaborate. Individuals with a personality profile characterized by high extraversion and high neuroticism have a more sensitive BIS, and therefore are more vulnerable to this technique. However, other profiles can also be triggered by this technique, especially sources with a high neuroticism.
