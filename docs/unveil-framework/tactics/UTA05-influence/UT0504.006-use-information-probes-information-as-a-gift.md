# UT0504.006 - Use information probes: Information as a gift

**Tactic:** [UTA05 - Influence](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may intentionally deliver non-binding information to the source with the expectancy of receiving additional information back. This technique leverages the principle of reciprocity, where individuals feel compelled to respond in kind when given something of value. By providing seemingly valuable, yet non-critical information, the handler can encourage the source to share more significant details. This technique involves the handler selecting pieces of information that appear useful or insightful but do not compromise the operation or the handler's safety. This could include sharing general knowledge, trivial insights related to the source's interests, or any other benign details. The key is to ensure that the information given is intriguing enough to prompt the source to reciprocate with their own information. For example, in a live conversation, the handler might share a recent industry rumor or a harmless personal anecdote that resonates with the source's interests or profession. In text-based interactions, the handler could send a message with a small piece of insider knowledge or an interesting fact, leaving the source curious and more likely to respond with more information.

The handler is trying to establish and maintain control over the source and the collection process. In this context, “to control” a source refers to the process of managing the relationship between the handler and the source, as well as the interactions between them. Therefore, the main goal of the handler during this tactic is to ensure that they are the one leading the source and not the other way around, which includes designing, representing and adapting a specific relationship with them. By no means the handler should lose control of the relationship and allow the source to dictate its terms, even if the strategy chosen by the handler and their team includes making the source feel in control, or even acting submissive to them. This tactic must be taken into account during the whole of the relationship, not only when approaching the source for the first time. Effective control also ensures that the handler can steer the interaction to meet strategic objectives while minimizing risks. It must be noted that the techniques in this tactic are designed to be executed combined with the ones in the UT05 Influence tactic.
