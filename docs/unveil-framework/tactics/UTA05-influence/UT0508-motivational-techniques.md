# UT0508 - Motivational techniques

**Tactic:** [UTA05 - Influence](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may use different variables in order to activate the different motivational systems to incentivize avoidance or approaching behaviors, as well as fight or flight actions. This technique focuses on the exploitation of the Behavioral Inhibition System (BIS), the Behavioral Activation System (BAS) and the Fight-Flight System (FFS). These systems can be linked to personality traits108, as people with a higher level of neuroticism and extraversion have a more active BAS, whereas the combination of high neuroticism and low extraversion (introversion) leads to a more active BIS. The FFS, however, has been linked to psychoticism, with individuals with lower scores in this trait showing a high activation of the FFS, which leads to a flight reaction, while high psychoticism people tend to show the fight responses associated with an infraactivation of the FFS.
