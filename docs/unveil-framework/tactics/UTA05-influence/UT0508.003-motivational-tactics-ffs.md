# UT0508.003 - Motivational tactics: FFS

**Tactic:** [UTA05 - Influence](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may employ tactics to activate the Fight - Flight System (FFS) in the source. Although this system is associated with responses to perceived threats or danger, in order to trigger it the handler does not need to evoke a great danger to the source, as smaller actions such as diminishing them or jump-scaring them would be enough to elicit a fight or flight response. For example, during an online chat, the handler could employ mild psychological stressors, such as diminishing the source's credibility or introducing sudden, unexpected questions to create a sense of urgency or discomfort. This could prompt the source to react defensively (fight) by trying to prove their reliability with more information, or to become overly compliant (flight) to quickly end the uncomfortable interaction, both of which can lead to the disclosure of valuable information. Considering how high psychoticism sources have a low FFS and, therefore, tend to react in Fight mode, this technique should be applied carefully to these kinds of profiles, especially when combined with a high extraversion and high neuroticism, which would make a rather impulsive individual.
