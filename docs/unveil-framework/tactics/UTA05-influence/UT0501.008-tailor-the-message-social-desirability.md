# UT0501.008 - Tailor the message:Social desirability

**Tactic:** [UTA05 - Influence](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may tailor their messages to the source's self monitoring level, that is, the level of adaptation of one's behavior to the social environment, with the objective of not being judged100. In particular, individuals with a high level of self monitoring tend to be more easily persuaded when the message highlights the social benefits of accepting a proposal or when it references the social validation principle, the tendency of individuals to conform to the actions or opinions of others in order to validate their own behavior or beliefs101. For example, if trying to persuade the individual to provide information, the handler could highlight how others in similar positions have already shared their insights, framing this behavior as socially desirable and widely accepted. By showcasing the social approval and potential benefits, such as increased respect and recognition within the community, the handler leverages the individual's tendency to adapt behavior for social acceptance, thereby increasing the likelihood of compliance.
