# UT0519 - Bait

**Tactic:** [UTA05 - Influence](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may elicit curiosity on the source by mentioning a topic they might be interested in but refusing to further elaborate on them intentionally, creating a sense of mystery and anticipation. This lack of elaboration is designed to prompt the target to take a specific action, such as responding to the handler or delivering more information. For example, a handler may throw a statement about a fact or action that is unknown for the source with the promise or revealing the information in a near future or after receiving something back. The technique's effectiveness will depend on to what extent the source is interested in the information used as a bait and on the capabilities of the handler to maintain the interest. In this regard, baits may not work equally with all psychological profiles. Note that the inability to match the source’s expectations, which may grow bigger with time, or pure bluffs may lead to significant rapport losses making it necessary to keep track of the historical acceptance of the source to put the technique into play.
