# UT0502 - Sender matching

**Tactic:** [UTA05 - Influence](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may design or adapt the persona's behavior and characteristics so that it matches with the source’́s, which will in turn enhance persuasion. For example, if the target is a military enthusiast active in online forums, the handler could design a persona that shares similar interests and background, such as a fellow military veteran who frequently posts knowledgeable comments and shares relevant articles. By mirroring the target's interests and demonstrating expertise in the same field, the handler’s persona becomes more relatable and credible, which enhances persuasion. Another way to implement this could be to carry out what is known as emotional mirroring, a technique in which the handler adapts their body language, movements, position, facial expressions or voice tone to mirror those of the source 105. This will, in turn, make the source unknowingly feel more comfortable with the handler. However, this technique can only be carried out in situations in which the handler and the source are seeing each other through life video. In order for this technique to work, it is necessary to adhere to the following process. 1. Mirror the source 2. Introduce a discriminative stimulus 3. Act so that the source will follow For example, a handler can effectively apply emotional mirroring during a live video interaction to build rapport and influence the source. If the source tends to use animated hand gestures and leans forward when speaking passionately, the handler can subtly mimic these behaviors. By aligning their body language, movements, and facial expressions with those of the source, the handler creates a sense of familiarity and comfort. Once rapport is established, the handler can introduce a discriminative stimulus, such as a specific topic or gesture, to gauge the source's reaction. By consistently mirroring the source and responding in a predictable manner, the handler can guide the source’s behavior and responses, enhancing their persuasive influence.
