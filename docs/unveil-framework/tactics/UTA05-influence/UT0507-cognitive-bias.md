# UT0507 - Cognitive bias

**Tactic:** [UTA05 - Influence](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may generate cognitive biases on the source in an attempt to influence them into thinking what would be more useful for the operation. Many cognitive biases can be used during the process of influencing a source. For example, the handler may increase the source's commitment by gradually escalating the level of involvement requested from the source, starting with small requests and gradually increasing their complexity or importance. By getting the source to commit to small tasks or actions initially, the handler can build momentum and increase the likelihood of the source agreeing to larger requests later on. This leverages the cognitive bias known as the "foot-in-the-door" phenomenon, where individuals are more likely to comply with a large request after agreeing to a smaller one. Human Intelligence Sources (CHIS)’, Journal of Policing, Intelligence and Counter Terrorism 18, no. 2 (3 April 2023): 158–73, https://doi.org/10.1080/18335330.2022.2108331.

Another example could be the employment of the confirmation bias, which can be triggered by providing the source with selective information that aligns with the source's pre-existing beliefs or suspicions. If the source already harbors some doubts about the target's loyalty, the handler can reinforce these doubts by presenting unverified anecdotes or leading questions that suggest disloyal behavior. This approach can make the source more inclined to share relevant information that they might otherwise withhold, under the belief that their insights are confirming the handler's narrative.
