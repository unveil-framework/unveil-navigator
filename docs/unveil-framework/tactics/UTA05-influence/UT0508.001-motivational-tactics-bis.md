# UT0508.001 - Motivational tactics: BIS

**Tactic:** [UTA05 - Influence](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may utilize techniques to activate the Behavioral Inhibition System (BIS) in the source. By triggering BIS, which is associated with avoidance behaviors and sensitivity to punishment, the handler aims to create a sense of apprehension or anxiety in the source. This may involve subtly highlighting potential risks or negative consequences of their actions, thereby motivating the source to engage in behaviors that minimize these perceived threats. For example, the handler could communicate with a target involved in questionable activities, gently reminding them of the legal and social repercussions if their actions were discovered. Individuals with a personality profile characterized by low extraversion and high neuroticism have a more sensitive BIS, and therefore are more vulnerable to this technique. However, other profiles can also be triggered by this technique, especially sources with a high neuroticism. This technique works well when paired with a negative reinforcement, that is, when, after generating higher levels of anxiety by activating the BIS, the handler reduces said anxiety, providing a reward for the source to associate the reward with the handler or to increase the probability of a specific behavior. Nonetheless, it can also be used to simply generate an avoidant behavior on the source.
