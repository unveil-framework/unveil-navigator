# UT0108.002 - Identify sources that need to be screened: Indirect source

**Tactic:** [UTA01 - Plan](./README.md)

## Description

The handler may identify indirect sources that need to be screened as part of the planning phase of the HUMINT operation. Indirect sources are individuals who may not possess the desired information themselves but have access to individuals or resources that do. This can include family members, colleagues, or associates of the direct source. Screening indirect sources involves evaluating their relationship with the direct source, their potential influence, and their ability to facilitate access to information. Identifying indirect sources that need to be screened allows the handler to expand their network and gather intelligence through secondary channels.

The handler is ensuring the protection of communication channels, data storage, and overall operational security (OPSEC) to support the operation's requirements. Given the cyber component of virtual HUMINT, technical safeguarding techniques regarding the protection of operational infrastructure must not be confused with general cybersecurity measures. The former involves implementing specific security practices essential for the agency’s operational integrity, while the latter may refer to broader IT security efforts. This focused safeguarding includes advanced encryption practices, secure handling and transmission of data, and counter-surveillance techniques. These measures are designed to prevent unauthorized access and leaks, ensuring that sensitive information is transmitted and stored securely to support the strategic objectives of the operation.
